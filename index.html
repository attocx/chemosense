Model Theory & Derivation

Technical framework for the "Realistic Dim Species" 1:1 binding model.

1. The 1:1 Binding Equation

Total fluorescence F is derived from the conservation of mass and the equilibrium dissociation constant (Kd). Expressed in initial conditions:

F = Fmax × [ 1 − (1 − α) ×
(I0 + H0 + Kd) − √((I0 + H0 + Kd)2 − 4I0H0)
2I0
]

2. Parameter Definitions

  • Fmax: Maximal fluorescence of free dye I.
  • Kd: Equilibrium dissociation constant.
  • α (Alpha): The residual fluorescence fraction of the bound complex (0 to 1).
  • Dynamic Range (DR): The maximum signal change, calculated as Fmax(1 - α).

3. Physical Interpretation

This model accounts for cases where the bound host-dye complex is not completely dark. The parameter α represents the "floor" of the assay, while the quadratic term ensures accuracy even when host concentrations are sub-stoichiometric relative to the dye.
